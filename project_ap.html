Understanding Media Bias through Actor Portrayals​r

Author(s): Ali Salloum, Yan Xia, Mikko Kivelä, Corinna Coupette

Abstract

Media bias significantly influences public perception, political discourse, and policy decisions, particularly in foreign news coverage. However, existing methods for studying media bias rely heavily on manual analysis and lack scalability and consistency. We introduce a computational framework for quantifying media bias through actor portrayals, capturing nuanced differences systematically and transparently. Our method extracts structured observations (subject-verb tuples) from news texts, annotates them with semantic metadata, and uses Bayesian multinomial distributions to quantify actor portrayals. Divergences between portrayals reveal distinct media biases, as demonstrated by analyzing portrayals of key actors in the Russia–Ukraine and Israel–Palestine conflicts across major outlets. This approach minimizes subjectivity, enhances reproducibility, and provides robust, large-scale comparative analysis across global media landscapes.
